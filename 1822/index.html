Our Story

In 1822, Stephen F. Austin led a small group of settlers into the Texas territory and granted land along the Brazos River to what history calls the Old 300 — the first Anglo colonists to put down roots in this state. Among them was Jared Ellison Groce, who rode alongside Austin, brought cotton seed to Texas soil, and built a plantation on the Brazos that became one of the young republic's most significant early homesteads.

Ric and Kevin Kiersh are Groce's fourth great-grandsons.

1822 Kitchen & Bar carries that name deliberately — as a marker of where this family's story in Texas begins.

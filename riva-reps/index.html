The Problem

You're Waiting for Business That Needs to Be Pursued.

Inbound marketing attracts customers. Direct business development wins contracts. Most local businesses only do one.

You have catering or contract capacity that's sitting idle

Your operation could handle corporate lunches, recurring service agreements, or bulk orders — but nobody knows unless someone already knows you. That pipeline doesn't build itself.

Your best accounts came from whoever you happened to know

Relationship-based deals built on personal connections are real — but they're not scalable. When your network dries up, so does that channel.

No systematic follow-up on warm or cold leads

You've probably had conversations that went nowhere not because the prospect wasn't interested — but because nobody followed up on a sequence. Most deals die in the follow-up gap.

No visibility into your pipeline or close rates

If you can't see what's in your pipeline, you can't manage it. Without tracking, you don't know if one deal is close to closing or if you're three months out from a revenue gap.

The biggest revenue opportunities in your market aren't waiting on Google. They require someone to go get them.

What We Do

Target Account Identification

Outreach Messaging

Follow-Up Sequence

CRM Setup and Pipeline Tracking

Pitch Materials

BD Representative Training

Train whoever is doing the outreach — owner, manager, or dedicated sales rep — on the targeting logic, messaging, objection handling, and pipeline management process.

How It Works

Five Steps. Active Pipeline in 30 Days.

1

Offer Clarification

Define exactly what you're selling to BD targets — catering packages, recurring service agreements, bulk orders, preferred vendor status — and what makes yours worth buying.

2

Prospect List Build

We research and compile a qualified list of 50–150 target accounts in your local market — with contact names, titles, and the specific reason they're a fit.

3

Outreach Launch

First-touch outreach begins across email, phone, or LinkedIn — starting with the highest-probability targets first and working down the list with a consistent cadence.

4

Follow-Up and Nurture

Interested prospects enter a multi-touch follow-up sequence — moving from initial contact to meeting to proposal with clear next steps at every stage.

5

Pipeline Review and Optimization

Monthly review of pipeline health — conversion rates by stage, which messaging is working, where deals are stalling — and adjustments to improve close rates over time.

Section 01

What We Thought We Were Building

"When we started, none of us fully knew what the engagement would become."

The original working assumption was straightforward. Riva Strategies would support social media, SEO, digital marketing, and general growth efforts, while Red River's internal team would eventually take on more of the routine social content.

The expectation was that the work would settle into a familiar rhythm, with managers participating more heavily in day-to-day content capture and execution over time.

  • Social media support
  • SEO support
  • Promotional planning
  • General digital marketing assistance
  • Website help when needed
  • Strategic guidance
Section 02

What We Discovered

Once we got inside the business, each improvement exposed the next connected need. The parts of Red River's digital presence didn't operate independently — they depended on each other.

"Every improvement exposed the next bottleneck."

  • Social media could create interest, but inaccurate menus or weak ordering paths reduced conversion.
  • Google Ads could drive traffic, but only if the website, profiles, and location targeting were accurate.
  • SEO could improve visibility, but only if the website and Google profiles contained consistent information.
  • Reviews affected trust, Maps visibility, advertising performance, and customer decisions.
  • Analytics was necessary to understand whether the work was producing useful outcomes.
  • Loyalty and ordering systems were necessary to turn attention into repeat business and revenue.
  • Video and social content were necessary to continually feed the system.
Section 03

Why Riva Took Control of the Whole System

Riva Strategies did not expand the work simply to create more work. The work expanded because the individual pieces could not perform properly unless the entire digital foundation was functioning.

"I was not trying to make the job bigger. I was trying to make the system work."

"I did not want to come back six months later and have everyone conclude that marketing did not work when the real issue was that half of the digital infrastructure had never been connected."

The original agreement became the working umbrella under which the broader digital operation was built. The priority was never tracking every task against a narrow contract — it was creating an ecosystem capable of producing results.

Section 04

The Eight-Valve Balloon

Think of Red River's digital presence as a balloon supported by eight valves. Every valve must receive air. When one is neglected, the entire system loses pressure.

Eight connected valves — each one a live pressure point

"I could not inflate the social media valve while leaving the website, Google, reviews, ads, and ordering systems flat."

  • Social media creates awareness and search activity.
  • Search sends customers to Google and the website.
  • Google profiles influence local visibility and trust.
  • Reviews affect conversion and Maps performance.
  • The website supports menus, ordering, SEO, advertising, and AI discovery.
  • Advertising depends on accurate locations, landing pages, offers, and data.
  • Analytics shows what is working and what is not.
  • Ordering and loyalty systems turn attention into measurable revenue.

Section 07

The Platform Learning Phase

"The learning phase was platform learning, not Jordan learning."

  • Google Ads needed time and data to understand audience behavior.
  • Meta needed engagement and response signals.
  • Google needed time to recrawl and reassess updated locations.
  • SEO changes required indexing.
  • Social platforms needed content history.
  • Analytics needed time to build usable baselines.
  • AI platforms needed stronger and more consistent source information.

The business is now moving out of the awkward setup and rebuilding phase. The system can become more organized, more efficient, and easier to manage.

"The work does not disappear. It shifts from building and repairing toward operating, optimizing, and improving."

Section 08

Why This Meeting Matters Now

Before the work began, no one had a clear picture of everything it would require. Now the ecosystem is visible, and Red River can make informed decisions about ownership.

  • What does Red River want Riva Strategies to own?
  • What does Red River want its internal team to own?
  • What systems need to be built so the work is repeatable and sustainable?
  • What work should happen continuously?
  • What work should happen only when requested?
  • What requires direct participation from managers and owners?
Section 09

Ownership Discussion

For each area below, mark who owns it: Riva Strategies, Red River, or Shared Responsibility. Selections carry into the printable summary at the end of this section.

Social Media

  • Does Riva Strategies own strategy, copy, editing, posting, and distribution?
  • Does Red River provide routine raw media?
  • Are managers expected to participate in content capture?
  • Is Riva expected to personally travel to locations for routine footage?

Website

  • Does Riva maintain and improve the website continuously?
  • Does Riva only make requested updates?
  • Who communicates menu, pricing, hours, and promotion changes?

Google Business Profiles

  • Does Riva own menus, photos, reviews, hours, descriptions, and optimization?
  • Who communicates operational changes?
  • Who monitors holiday hours and closures?

SEO & AI Discoverability

  • Does Red River want ongoing improvement?
  • Does Red River want periodic maintenance?
  • Does Red River want the current foundation maintained without continued expansion?

Paid Advertising

  • Does Riva own Google and Meta advertising decisions?
  • Who approves offers and budgets?
  • Who provides timely promotional details and creative assets?

Reviews & Reputation

  • Does Riva respond to reviews?
  • Does management handle sensitive complaints?
  • Who alerts Riva to operational issues that appear in reviews?

Analytics & Reporting

  • Does Red River want recurring reporting?
  • Does Red River want performance summaries only when requested?
  • Who reviews the data and makes operational decisions?

Menus, Ordering & Loyalty

  • Does Riva own digital accuracy?
  • Does management communicate every menu change?
  • Who confirms pricing, availability, and ordering links?
  • Does Red River want additional loyalty systems built?

Content Production

  • Are managers responsible for routine footage and photos?
  • Should professional shoots be scheduled?
  • Should Riva build a repeatable manager content process?
  • What level of production is expected from Jordan personally?

System Overview

Purpose of This System

This page is the internal operational reference for Red River Cantina social media management. It defines how the system runs, what operators should prioritize, and how content should be created and executed across both locations.

This is not a marketing brief. It is an operating standard. Use it to stay consistent, stay on-brand, and support real daily visibility — not trend chasing.
Brand Foundation

Concept Positioning

Red River Cantina is a social, energetic, welcoming Tex-Mex restaurant concept focused on drinks, atmosphere, entertainment, food, and community familiarity. The brand should feel active, local, consistent, authentic, and experience-driven.

Core Essence: Modern Mexican cantina. Coastal Texas Tex-Mex. Vibrant food, handcrafted drinks, and warm hospitality in an energetic, festive atmosphere.

Events are amplification — not the foundation. Normal daily operations, Happy Hour visibility, social atmosphere, drinks, entertainment, and customer familiarity are the true long-term drivers. Cinco de Mayo is a volume booster, not the content strategy.

Day-to-Day Operations

Daily Execution Standards

1
Verify before you post

Confirm specials, pricing, times, and event details are current and accurate. Never post outdated information.

2
Identify the strongest opportunity for the day

Happy Hour? A special? Atmosphere? Patio? Pick the one content angle that serves the day's best visibility goal.

3
Use real, current media

Pull from the active media library. No outdated photos. No AI-generated food or drink imagery. Ever.

4
Build stories for vertical mobile viewing

Stories are not resized wall posts. Build them native. Keep them clean, readable, and quick to scan.

5
Write copy that sounds like a person, not a brand deck

Active, local, social tone. No corporate language. No overhyping routine operations.

6
Publish on schedule

Consistency matters more than timing perfection. Post regularly throughout the week. Don't stack posts or go dark for days.

7
Increase frequency on high-traffic days

Friday, Saturday, and major event days get extra stories. Don't overwhelm — but don't miss peak windows either.

Happy Hour gets dedicated visibility multiple times per week. Priority days: Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri, Sat. Show drinks, bar atmosphere, patio energy, and specials. Feels ongoing and social — never like a coupon ad.
Top Traffic Driver Tue–Sat Stories + Wall
04
Week Ahead SOP
Week Ahead content is posted early in the week to set expectations and prime visibility. Covers upcoming specials, entertainment, events, and recurring promotions. Helps customers plan ahead and reinforces routine.
Monday Push Planning Tool
05
Event Amplification SOP
Events amplify the existing system — they don't replace it. Pre-event announcements, countdown stories, real-time atmosphere content, and post-event recaps. Always transition back to normal cadence after an event wraps.
Amplification Only Pre + Live + Recap
06
Cross-Posting SOP
High-value content gets shared across both location pages. Always verify location accuracy, adjust captions when needed, and avoid over-duplication. Strategic use only — not automatic syndication.
Both Locations Strategic Use Only
07
Reel & Video SOP
Video content should capture real atmosphere, real energy, and real customer experience. Short-form, mobile-optimized, authentic. Not polished brand reels. Not stock footage. Not manufactured social moments.
Real Footage Only Mobile-First
08
Community Engagement SOP
Monitor tags, comments, and shares. Respond consistently. Highlight positive customer moments. Support local events and partnerships when brand-aligned. Keep all public responses welcoming and professional.
Ongoing Local Connection
09
Visual Standards SOP
Real food. Real drinks. Real atmosphere. Real customer activity. Brand colors, typography, and layout rules must be followed exactly. No AI imagery. No fake architecture. No clip art. No hard-to-read text.
Non-Negotiable Brand Kit Required
10
Website Update SOP
Website should always match social. Specials, Happy Hour, events, hours, and menus stay current. Remove expired promotions immediately. Mobile readability is required. Consistency between web and social builds customer trust.
Accuracy Required Sync with Social

Copy Direction

CTA Standards

Calls to action should feel natural and social — not like a promotional email. Direct, active, and local. No overhyping. No corporate sales language.

✓ Use This
Happy Hour starts at 4. Come find us on the patio.
✗ Not This
Don't miss out on our AMAZING deals — LIMITED TIME ONLY!!!
✓ Use This
Fajita Wednesday is today. $24.95 combo starting at 4.
✗ Not This
🚨 HUGE SAVINGS on the BEST FAJITAS in Texas 🚨 Don't wait!
✓ Use This
$5 margaritas all day Thursday. See you there.
✗ Not This
An experience you will NEVER forget. Book your table NOW!!!
Always verify all pricing and details before publishing any CTA. Inaccurate pricing or times is one of the most damaging errors in the system.
Content Guardrails

Anti-AI Formatting Guidance

The Cantina system has hard rules against artificial or misleading content. These apply to all content types, all platforms, and all operators.

  • No AI-generated food or drink imagery — ever. It misrepresents the real product.
  • No fake or altered building architecture — use real photos of the actual locations only.
  • No overproduced graphics that make the Cantina feel like a corporate chain.
  • No manufactured social moments — real customer energy, real atmosphere.
  • No trend-chasing content that lacks operational purpose or brand alignment.
  • No nightclub-style marketing unless specifically event-driven and approved.
  • No inaccurate pricing, times, or event information in any content.
  • No low-quality, blurry, or outdated media in any post or story.
System Rules

Non-Negotiables

These cannot be compromised.
  • No inconsistent posting behavior during standard operating weeks
  • No off-brand visuals or messaging on any platform
  • No random content posting without an operational purpose
  • No excessive dependence on major events for engagement
  • No misleading representation of the customer experience
  • No sacrificing consistency in exchange for trend chasing
  • No inaccurate pricing, promotions, or event information
  • No AI-generated food or drink imagery

Operator Workflow

Social Operations Workflow

This section defines how operators and managers interact with the social operations system on a day-to-day basis.

→
Start of week: Pull the Week Ahead

Review what specials, events, and entertainment are scheduled. Set the content calendar for the week. Don't improvise daily.

→
Daily check: Verify before publishing

Always confirm current specials, times, and event details are accurate before any story or wall post goes live.

→
Media: Pull from the current library

Use only approved, current real photography. If the library needs updated content, flag it and schedule a capture session.

→
Community: Monitor and respond consistently

Check tags, comments, and messages. Respond promptly. Keep tone welcoming and professional — not corporate or scripted.

→
Events: Execute the amplification protocol

For any major event, follow the Event Amplification SOP. Begin pre-event visibility early. Capture real-time content during the event. Post recap content after.

→
Website: Keep it synced with social

Any specials, event listings, or hours posted on social should match the website exactly. Remove expired promotions immediately.

→
Escalation: Flag anything that needs approval

New pricing, significant promotions, large event partnerships, or any off-brand content requests get reviewed before publishing.

Monetization Framework

Revenue Support Cadence

Visibility content builds familiarity. Revenue content captures intent. Both systems must coexist inside the same operational cadence without either one displacing the other. The Cantina brand should never feel like it's selling — but it should always be converting.

Visibility Content
Builds familiarity over time. Atmosphere, Happy Hour, food, drinks, entertainment. The engine of long-term customer behavior.
Revenue Content
Captures intent when it exists. Delivery, direct ordering, packs, catering. Integrated — never the lead.
Coexistence Rule
Both systems run simultaneously. Revenue content layers into existing windows. It does not replace cadence posts.
Authenticity Standard
Never sacrifice brand feel for conversion pressure. No fake urgency. No CTA stacking. No AI-generated marketing energy.
Revenue content should feel like a natural extension of the atmosphere — not a departure from it. If a post reads like an ad, it's not right.

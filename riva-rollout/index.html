The Problem

You Post When You Remember. That's Not a Strategy.

Inconsistency is the most common and most expensive marketing problem for local businesses.

Three posts last month. Zero the month before.

Algorithms and audiences both reward consistency. An inconsistent presence gets deprioritized — and people forget you exist between visits.

You rely on one person to "do the social media"

When that person leaves, gets busy, or burns out — marketing stops. There's no system. It's a dependency. Dependencies create gaps.

Content is reactive, not planned

You post about things after they happen instead of building anticipation. Last-minute "specials tonight" posts don't move people who already booked dinner somewhere else.

You're creating content that doesn't drive decisions

Likes don't pay bills. Content needs to give people a specific reason to come in, call, or book — not just scroll past.

A content system isn't about volume. It's about showing up predictably with content that influences the next decision.
